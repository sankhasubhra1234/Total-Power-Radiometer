    Michael Hann • 2 months ago • edited

    Has anyone succeeded yet in building and operating the radio telescope described in the article? I have built the antenna, assembled the electronics, and installed the HDSDR software . . . but I have not succeeded in capturing the 21 cm H I emission. I feel I am probably making some lame-brained mistake in my set-up -- either in operating the SAWbird LNA/SAW device or in operating the HDSDR software. There are no instructions for using the HDSDR software, other than GUI prompts when hovering over controls, which are helpful enough but may not get me all the way there.

    The LNA/SAW device is receiving power from my NESDR SMArTee XTR SDR (note, I did use the variant outputting Bias Tee power on the coax), as evidenced by the LED on the LNA/SAW lighting up brightly. I assume there is no need to control the switch related to the calibration 50 ohm reference termination -- I assume this defaults with the 50 ohm reference termination out of the circuit and the input (from my horn antenna) in the circuit.

    I have determined when the galactic equator is directly overhead and pointed my horn antenna at that: no 21 cm H I detection (there should be more neutral hydrogen in the antenna beam when pointing into the plane of the galaxy than when pointing out of the plane of the galaxy). I have pointed my horn antenna at the sun. No distinct change in the signal received. I have operated the system (A) with the horn antenna and LNA/SAW in the circuit and (B) with the horn antenna and LNA/SAW out of the circuit and found no difference in the spectrum display on the HDSDR display. I do see a peak at the local oscillator frequency, which is a spurious "signal" which I merely disregard.

          Martin Blustine • 4 months ago

          Thanks for a very well written article. I could not help but notice that the online link in the article that references the dongle takes me to the Nooelec site for the NESDR SMArt XTR SDR which does not contain a bias-T. A bias-T is useful for powering the preamp/SAW gizmo through its SMA output connector, as referenced in the gizmo link.

          I believe that the correct link should be for the NESDR SMArTee XTR SDR at https://www.nooelec.com/sto...
          Also, please note that the SAWbird+ H1 Barebones gizmo has an input switch that can toggle the preamp input between the antenna and a 50 ohm reference termination.

          The gizmo has a stated noise figure in the range of 0.9 and 1.3 dB, or 66.8K and 101.2K, respectively.

          The switch loss will directly increase the noise figure of the device over that of the SAWbird+ H1, according to the data sheet, but the switch loss has not been specified. When I tried to find a switchless SAWbird+ H1 for the frequency of interest, I could not locate one for sale.
